Projects & Competitions

Gauge-Equivariant U-Net for Generative Models

Master's Thesis

Designed and implemented a gauge-equivariant U-Net in PyTorch for generative modeling on SU(N) lattice gauge theories. The architecture encodes physical symmetry constraints directly into the network, enabling diffusion models and continuous normalizing flows to efficiently sample from high-dimensional distributions — a scalable alternative to traditional Markov Chain Monte Carlo methods. Work led to a submitted paper with the ETH High Performance Computational Physics group (arXiv:2605.06134).

BKT Phase Transition Simulation

Project

Implemented the Wolff cluster Monte Carlo algorithm to simulate the 2D classical XY model and characterise the Berezinskii–Kosterlitz–Thouless phase transition. Computed physical observables including spin stiffness (helicity modulus), susceptibility, and vortex density; performed finite-size scaling analysis to estimate the transition temperature. Optimised for performance using Numba.

Research

Diffusion model for SU(N) gauge theories

Submitted · May 2026 · arXiv:2605.06134

Presents a score-matching framework for training diffusion models on SU(N) lattice gauge theories. Applied to SU(3) gauge configurations in two and four dimensions; generated samples validated against Hybrid Monte Carlo simulations. For high inverse coupling, predictor-corrector schemes with Hamiltonian molecular dynamics corrections improve sampling quality. Part of an international collaboration led by the ETH Zurich High Performance Computational Physics group.
